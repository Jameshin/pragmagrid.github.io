Expeditions and Technology Development

PRAGMA forges collaborative, multidisciplinary teams to address scientific questions of high societal impact. These scientific questions define and drive PRAGMA's technology development.

There are three current expeditions:

  • Virtual Biodiversity: Understanding adaption in extreme environments;
       Aimee Stewart (U. Kansas) and Nadya Williams (UCSD)
  • Lake Sciences: Predicting impact of eutrophication on lake ecosystem services;
       Paul Hanson (U. Wisc), Cayelan Carey (Virginia Tech) and Renato Figueiredo (U. Florida)
  • PRAGMA-ENT: Creating and utilizing an Experimental software-defined Network Testbed;
       Kohei Ichikawa, (NAIST), Matt Collins (U. Florida)

Technology Development:

  • Creating a multi-cloud environment through application-based migration experiments
      Participants: Phil Papadopoulos (UCSD), Yoshio Tanaka (AIST), and Weicheng Huang (NCHC)
  • Building a PRAGMA Data Cloud through data sharing, provenance, data valuation and evolution experiments
       Participant: Beth Plale (Indiana U)
  • Developing a trust envelope for collaboration through software-defined overlay networks and experiments with IPv6;
      Participants: Jose Fortes and Renato Figueriedo (U Florida), Kohei Ichikawa, (NAIST)
         Weicheng Huang (NCHC)

Working Groups

RESOURCES WORKING GROUP: Working to make the distributed resources of PRAGMA useful to diverse applications. Co-leaders: Yoshio Tanaka (AIST) and Phil Papadopoulos (UCSD)

TELESCIENCE WORKING GROUP: Focusing on a variety of activities that require access to, or use of, remote equipment, such as tiled-display walls (TDW) and sensors. Co-leaders: Shinji Shimojo (NICT and Osaka U) and Fang-Pang Lin (NCHC)

BIOSCIENCES WORKING GROUP: Focusing much of its efforts over the last several years on integrating technologies to create an infrastructure to advance the screening of potential compounds to combat infectious diseases. Leader: Jason Haga (AIST)

CYBERLEARNING: Propagating EDISON and Computational Science from KISTI to NCHC. Leader: Ruth Lee (KISTI)

Governance

PRAGMA is led by a Steering Committee, which operate according to the Operating Principles and Procedures document, which all PRAGMA members agree to abide by. The Steering Committee sets direction, reviews membership applications and proposals to host PRAGMA workshops.
